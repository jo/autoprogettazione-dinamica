Autoprogetazione

"Autoprogetazione" ist der Titel eines Buches (978-88-87942-67-5) von Enzo Mari, Architekt und Künstler. Der Einleitungstext:

PROPOSTA PER UN'AUTOPROGETTAZIONE A project for making easy-to-assemble furniture using rough boards and nails. An elementary technizue to teach anyone to look at present production with a critical eye. (Anyone, apart from factories and traders, can use these designs to make them by themselves. The author hopes the idea will last into the future and asks those who build the furniture, and in particular, variations of it, to send photos to his studio at 10 piazzale Baracca, 10 - 29123 Milan). © project Enzo Mari, 1974

Open Source Design for Everybody

Das ist Open Source für Möbel. Das hier ist Autoprogettazione Dinamica, auch Open Source (also bitte: mach mit!). Ich habe mir einen Bauplan rausgenommen, der mir am einfachsten erschien: 1123xB, zu finden auf Seite 12. Das ist ein solider Tisch, mit Diagonalen in beiden Horizontalen versteift. Der Tisch besteht aus drei verschiedenen Teilen: Der Tischplatte, zwei Seitenteilen rechts und links, und einem Kiel. Der Kiel wird in die Seitenteile gesteckt und die Tischplatte aufgelegt. Latten verhindern eine Verrutschen.

Adaptation & Dynamics

Mit dieser Webapp, konfigurierst Du Dir Deinen idealen Tisch zusammen. Neben Länge, Breite und Höhe passt Du auch auch den Lattenquerschnitt an konkrete Verfügbarkeiten und Gegebenheiten an. Ein Puppentischen aus Streichhölzern oder ein riesiger Zelttisch aus dicken Bohlen. Mit der richtigen Konfiguration wird der Tisch zur Bank, die sich in jede Nische einpasst. Oder zum Stehpult, für Deinen Rücken.

Construction Plan

Du druckst Dir Konstruktionspläne und eine Stückliste aus. Dann sägst Du Dir die Längen zu, oder lässt das im Baumarkt erledigen. Anhand des Bauplans und der Anleitung nagelst Du die Latten aufeinander. Du kannst sie auch verschrauben oder verleimen.

Owning the Means of Production

If you, like me, don't own a professional woodworking shop with a saw table etc., then you rely on material, that you can process with the equipment you have at home. I had bought myself a chop & miter saw and from then on I was able to cut boards and battens up to 10cm wide (and maybe 4cm thick). I construct everything from boards and laths since then. The lath is per-se the raw material as it comes rough sawn from the sawmill.

Dig into the Things

Furniture design shaped by the material and its manufacturing process is design based on possibility, it is material driven design.

We surround ourselves with an increasing number of products with decreasing useful life. Production usually happens far away, hidden. The only touchpoint with the origin of our product is the parcel carrier.

And maybe a website for the ambitious products-nerd and here we are getting closer.

The researched product is in its observation enriched by background information. Maybe about its manufacture, or about special culture of the company, or whatever. In any case, you have spent time with the product, setting up resonance centers, developing topics. And all this resonates when you look at the researched product from now on.

The self-built product has a haptic memory attachment. Its perception is strong and permanently impregnated with an awareness of its fabrication. Maybe you hurt your thumb. It makes you recognize the product in its entire four-dimensionality, interwoven with your personal life.

The self-designed product however lets us experience the world as hackable, as modifiable. The self-designed product invites us to a dialogue with the whole world of products. Active construction and design train us in a critical approach to products.
